King Safety

Open g-file

White plays and wins. Black has an exposed king and an inactive rook. White needs to keep black busy, or the tables could be turned due to blacks material advantage.

Once white starts giving check the game is probably easily won; but as long as white can only keep the pressure on with threats other than a check, they must be careful not to get checks themselves, or white might cause lose the initiative.

The only winning move therefore is 1. Kf1! This unpins the white rook and threatens Rg4+ with a swift end in favour of white. Black can't give a check that makes sense, so they play 1. ... Qh5 to stop Rg4. And again white only has a single winning move to win! Note there are few interesting variations here: 1. ... h5?! leads to a mating net: 2. Qg6+! Kh8 3. Qh6+ Kg8 4. Bd3! and the threat Qh7# is too strong. For example: 4. ... Re1+!? 5. Kxe1 Qc3+ 6. Ke2! (6. Kf2?? Qxd4+ and 6. Kf1 Qa1+ 7. Ke2 Re8+ 8. Kf3 Qd1+ and white can't escape infinite check: 9. Kd3 Qe1+ 10. Kf3 Qd1 etc. (10. Kh3?? Re3+ 11. g3? Rxg3!! hxg3 Qh1#) and 6. Kd1 Qa1+ with a similar results.) 6. ... Re8+ 7. Kf3 Qc6+ and black had to give a rook to avoid mate by trading queens, but still loses the end game.

Note that 2. h3?? loses the game to 2. ... Re6!!. while 2. ... Rd3?? loses to 2. ... Qe2+. The only winning move is 2. Rd5 : this renews the threat (Rg5+) while stopping black from giving checks. 2. ... Qg6 3. Rg5 Re6 4. Rxg6+ hxg6 5. Qg5 and white wins (5. Bxe6?? fxe6 oops).
