The fianchetto without bishop (continued)

The main difference between the previous two moves and the remaining two moves is that with the queen on f2 or f3, Nf5 would block the queen from going to f6 and black had Qe7 as defense. Neither are possible if white goes to the d8-h4 diagonal. For example, 1. Qh4 Nf5?? 2. Qf6 and again black has to give up a knight to stop the impending mate. E.g. 2. ... Nbd4 3. Rxd4!. Obviously black can't take the rook or whites bishop takes back with mate in two, but the rook is blocking the bishop, which gives black one extra tempo to avoid immediate mate with 3. ... Qe7 4. Qxe7 Nxe7 5. Rd7 and white is a piece up.

Nevertheless, also 1. Qh4 isn't the winning move as black can now defend with 1. ... Nd6! (only move) 2. Qf6 Nde8 where one knight defends g7 while the other blocks the white queen from going to h8! And since the white queen is attacked as well, he doesn't have time to threaten to sacrifice the rook on e8: 3. Qc3 h5! 4. Re1 Kh7 and the black rook is now defending h8.

This Nd6 resource does not work with the winning move: 1. Qg5! (i.e. 1. ... Nd6 2. Bxg7!! Ne4 (2. ... Kxg7 3. Qe5+ and white wins the knight) 3. Qh6 (also 3. Qe3 and 3. Qh4 win) 3. ... Nf2+ 4. Kg1 Ng4 (4. ... Nxd1?? 5. Bd4 and mate in 3) 5. Qh4 Kxg7 6. Qxg4 and white is again a piece up).

Finally we have to look at 1. ... Ne8. As we saw before, this isn't a good defense because it only defends f6, while having the white queen anywhere on the long diagonal wins. For example, 2. a4 would again win the knight as 2. Nd6? Qe5 is mate in two. But even better is 2. h4!. Since the knight left g7, h5 is now deadly weakened. There is nothing black can do against the threat h4-h5-hxg7. Note that none of blacks pieces can move: the knight on e8 has to defend f6, the queen on c7 has to defend e5 and the knight on b5 can't go to d6 because then it blocks the queen from defending e5 (this is also why Qb8 doesn't help). For example what could follow is 2. ... a6? with the idea that now at least a4 can be met with Na7. 3. h5 Na7 basically doing nothing, but what is white going to do? 4. hxg5 Nc8 5. gxf7#. Of course black has better, but everything involves sacrificing at least knight.

By the way, after 1. Qg5 Ne8 white also can play 2. Ba1! and then move the queen to b2: the only undefended square on the long diagonal. Try it out!
