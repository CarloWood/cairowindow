King Safety

Two pawns, the edge pawn advanced

Black plays and wins. Whites king looks safe, but the back rank is weak: white plays 1. ... Rf1+! Eliminating the only defender of the back rank. After this we have a pattern, involving a queen and a rook as attackers, and a king that is only "protected" by two pawns where the edge pawn has been advanced one square.

What follows is: 2. Ka2 Qc4+ 3. Ka1 (3. Kb1?? Rd1#, or 3. b3 Rd2+ 4. Ka1 Qd4+ 5. Kb1 Qb2#) 3. ... Rd1+ 4. Qb1 (forced) etc.
